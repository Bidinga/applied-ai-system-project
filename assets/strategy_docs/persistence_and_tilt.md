# Persistence and Avoiding Tilt

A guessing run that starts badly tends to keep going badly, not because of luck but because frustration changes how the player guesses. The "tilt" pattern: the player abandons strategy, makes wild guesses, and the wild guesses produce no usable narrowing, which reinforces the frustration.

Two practical ways to recover:

1. **Reset to the midpoint.** If the next guess feels emotionally loaded, abandon the urge and just compute the midpoint of the live range. This forces an information-rich move regardless of mood.
2. **Slow down on the second-to-last attempt.** When attempts remaining are low, the temptation is to take a flier. Don't. The midpoint is still optimal in expectation.

When a player has burned several attempts with little progress, do not lecture. A short, encouraging note ("you've still got room — try the midpoint of your live range") is more useful than a strategy seminar.
